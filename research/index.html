Evaluating Short- and Long-Term Moving Average Strategies on SPY

Our first research paper explores one of the most widely used tools in trading: the moving average. While moving averages are often seen as "basic," their design choices can make a profound difference when tested over long horizons.

In this study, we compared two strategies applied to nearly two decades of SPY data (2006‚Äì2025). The first model, CX-01, relied on a 20-day moving average, producing frequent trades that responded quickly to market shifts. The second model, CX-1.1, used the well-known Golden Cross / Death Cross (50/200-day crossover) combined with a 20-day filter, trading less often but aligning more closely with long-term market trends.

Results ‚Äî the trade-off:

  • CX-01: 500+ trades, high win rate, modest profit per trade; struggled in downturns.
  • CX-1.1: <20 trades, larger gains per trade, stronger terminal value; robust in 2008 & 2020 crises.

Faster models capture more opportunities but face noise; slower models trade less but compound more steadily. This work sets the foundation for adaptive moving averages, risk-adjusted performance, and multi-asset analysis.
